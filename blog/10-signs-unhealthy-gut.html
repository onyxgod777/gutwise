10 Signs Your Gut Is Out of Balance (And What to Do About It)

May 4, 2026 · 6 min read · ← Blog

Your gut is home to trillions of bacteria — collectively called your microbiome — that influence everything from digestion and immunity to mood and energy levels. When this delicate ecosystem falls out of balance (a state called dysbiosis), your whole body feels it.

The challenge? Many people don't realize their gut is struggling until the symptoms become hard to ignore. Here are 10 common warning signs that your gut may need attention.

1. Chronic Bloating and Gas

Occasional bloating after a heavy meal is normal. But if you feel bloated most days — distended, uncomfortable, and passing excessive gas — it's a strong signal that your gut bacteria are out of balance. An overgrowth of certain bacteria can produce excess gas during fermentation, leading to that constant "full" feeling.

2. Unexplained Fatigue

Your gut produces neurotransmitters that regulate energy. When the microbiome is compromised, nutrient absorption suffers — especially B vitamins and iron — both critical for energy production. If you're sleeping enough but still dragging through the day, your gut could be the culprit.

3. Brain Fog and Poor Concentration

The gut-brain axis is a bidirectional communication network linking your digestive system to your central nervous system. An unhealthy gut sends inflammatory signals to the brain, impairing focus, memory, and mental clarity. If your thinking feels "foggy," look downstream.

4. Sugar Cravings That Won't Quit

Certain gut bacteria thrive on sugar and send signals to your brain to crave more of it. The more sugar you eat, the more those bacteria multiply — creating a vicious cycle. If you constantly crave sweets, it may not be willpower that's failing; it's your microbiome calling the shots.

5. Unintentional Weight Changes

Gaining or losing weight without changing your diet or activity level can be linked to your gut. Some bacteria are more efficient at extracting calories from food, while others influence fat storage. An imbalanced microbiome can disrupt your metabolism in ways that defy conventional calorie counting. Learn more about the gut-weight connection →

6. Skin Irritations (Acne, Eczema, Rosacea)

Your skin is a mirror of your internal health. When the gut lining becomes inflamed or "leaky," toxins and undigested food particles can enter the bloodstream, triggering inflammatory skin conditions. Clearing up chronic skin issues often starts in the gut, not on the surface.

7. Food Intolerances

Developing new sensitivities to foods you once tolerated well — dairy, gluten, eggs, or certain vegetables — is a classic sign of gut imbalance. When beneficial bacteria decline, the gut lining can become compromised, making it harder to digest certain foods and more likely to react to them.

8. Irregular Bowel Movements

Whether it's constipation, diarrhea, or alternating between the two, irregularity is one of the most direct signs your digestive system is struggling. Healthy bowel movements should be regular, well-formed, and easy to pass — once or twice daily.

9. Weakened Immunity

Approximately 70% of your immune system resides in your gut. When your microbiome is out of balance, your immune defenses weaken. If you're catching every cold that goes around or taking longer than usual to recover from illness, your gut may need support.

10. Mood Swings and Anxiety

The gut produces about 90% of your body's serotonin — the "happiness" neurotransmitter that regulates mood, sleep, and emotional balance. When gut health declines, serotonin production drops, contributing to mood swings, irritability, and anxiety. As the ancient wisdom goes: heal the gut, calm the mind.

What to Do About It

If several of these signs resonate, here's where to start:

  • Eat whole foods — prioritize fiber-rich vegetables, fruits, and fermented foods
  • Hydrate properly — water supports digestion and nutrient absorption
  • Reduce sugar and processed foods — these feed harmful bacteria
  • Support your gut with natural supplements — certain herbal blends and teas can help restore balance
  • Prioritize sleep and stress management — both directly impact the microbiome
